Website Planning Document: The Thrills of Benin Bronzes for Kids

Site Name

The site is named Benin Bronzes for Kids because the name helps to make the site inviting for kids, teachers, and parents who want to learn of or teach the history and culture of the Benin people in a fun and engaging manner.

Site Purpose

This website helps children learn about the Benin Bronzes — ancient African artworks, from the Kingdom of Benin — in an interactive, easy-to-understand way. Through colorful visuals, simplified stories, and games, it introduces the history, techniques, and cultural significance of these bronzes.

Scenarios

These are questions that the target audience (primary school children, parents, or teachers) might ask when visiting the site:

  • How do I properly teach my child about Benin Bronzes?
  • Where and when were the Benin Bronzes made and what do they show?
  • How were these amazing artworks created- the smithing technique?
  • Why are many Benin Bronzes in museums far away from Benin?
  • Can I play games or quizzes to learn more about Benin Bronzes?
  • Will these Benin Bronzes ever be returned to Benin?

Color Schema

The website will use bright and cheerful color palette to appeal to young children while maintaining readability:

  • Primary Color: Bright Blue (#1E90FF) - Used for headers, buttons, and accents to create a friendly and engaging scenes
  • Secondary Color: Gentle Coral Pink (#ff6b81) - Used for backgrounds and highlights to evoke health and vitality.

These colors are applied for consistency.

Typography

The website will use the following fonts to ensure readability and a kid friendly aesthetic:

  • Font 1: Baloo Bhai - Used for headings (h1, h2, h3) to create a playful and cheerful look that appeals to children.
  • Font 2: Roboto - Used for body text to ensure readability for both kids and adults.
